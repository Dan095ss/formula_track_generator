Inspect battery carrier for damage caused by loss of acid from battery. If acid damage is present, it will be necessary to clean area with a solution of clean warm water and baking soda. Scrub area with a stiff bristle brush and wipe off with a cloth moistened with ammonia or baking soda in water.

Clean top of battery with same solutions as described in step 3.

Inspect battery case and cover for cracks. If cracks are present, battery must be replaced.

Clean the battery post with a suitable battery post cleaning tool.

Clean the inside surfaces of the terminal clamps with a suitable battery terminal cleaning tool. Replace damaged or frayed cables and broken terminal clamps.

Install the battery in vehicle.
